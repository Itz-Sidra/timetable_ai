How It Works

01 / EXPAND

Sessions are expanded from subject hours — theory, lab (2 consecutive), tutorial per division

02 / SORT

LAB sessions scheduled first (hardest), then tutorials, then theory — MRV heuristic

03 / DFS

Depth First Search assigns (teacher, room, timeslot) to each session across all 7 divisions

04 / BACKTRACK

On constraint violation, algorithm backtracks and tries next valid combination

AI Syllabus — ML2308

Artificial Intelligence · SY CSE (AI&ML) · A.Y. 2025-26

UNIT I
Fundamentals of Artificial Intelligence
5 Hours
Introduction: A.I. Representation, Non-AI & AI Techniques, Knowledge Base Systems, State Space Search, Production Systems, Problem Characteristics, Types of production systems, Turing Test.
Intelligent Agents: Agents and Environments, Rationality, Nature of Environments, Structure of Agents, Problem Solving Agents.
Problem Formulation: Vacuum World, 8 Queens, Route Finding, Robot Navigation.
UNIT II
Uninformed Search Strategies
5 Hours
Depth First Search, Breadth First Search, Depth Limited Search, Iterative Deepening DFS, Bidirectional Search, Comparison of Uninformed Search Strategies.
UNIT III
Informed Search Methods
5 Hours
Generate & Test, Hill Climbing, Best First Search, A* and AO* Algorithm, Constraint Satisfaction, Means Ends Analysis.
Game Playing: Minimax Search, Alpha-Beta Cutoffs, Quiescence Search.
UNIT IV
Logical Agents
5 Hours
Knowledge Based Agents, Wumpus World.
Propositional Logic: Representation, Inference, Reasoning Patterns, Resolution, Forward and Backward Chaining.
First Order Logic: Representation, Inference, Reasoning Patterns, Resolution, Forward and Backward Chaining.
UNIT V
Basics of PROLOG
4 Hours
Representation, Structure, Backtracking.
Expert System: Design, Implementation, Case Study of Expert System in PROLOG.
UNIT VI
Planning
4 Hours
Blocks World, STRIPS, Goal Stack Implementation.
State Space Search: Forward, Backward, Heuristics.
Partial Order Planning, Planning Graphs, Hierarchical Planning, Conditional Planning, Continuous Planning.
